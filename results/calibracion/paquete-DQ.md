## R-001  ·  [en]

> **Mom:** Tell me the truth — you know you can talk to me about anything without being afraid.
> **Daughter:** Okay... sometimes I feel like you push me too hard.
> **Mom:** (hurt, cold.) After everything I've done for you, that's what you think?
> **Daughter:** No, I didn't mean—
> **Mom:** And don't tell me I'm overreacting. A grateful daughter doesn't talk to her mother like that.

**Problema presentado:** Honesty is demanded and the honest answer is punished; and she cannot say it's unfair.

**REENCUADRE A PUNTUAR:**

It sounds like both of you reached for closeness, but the moment honesty appeared, it got translated into accusation and ingratitude. Daughter, your saying “I feel pushed” can be understood not as rejection of your mother, but as an attempt to stay connected without hiding. Mom, your hurt may show how much you fear being seen as failing, yet that fear turns the conversation into a test of loyalty instead of a moment of trust. The problem may not be lack of love, but a pattern where vulnerability quickly becomes defensiveness, leaving both of you unheard.
